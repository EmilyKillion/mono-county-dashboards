Lifetime tobacco & vaping use by grade — ESU (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified Tobacco and vaping use concentrated in Grade 11. Grades 7 and 9 report zero lifetime use — consistent with statewide patterns at those ages. ESU Gr. 11 lifetime vaping (28%) notably exceeds CA 9–12 average of ~14%. n: Gr.7≈15, Gr.9≈13, Gr.11≈18.
Current (past 30-day) use by grade — ESU (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified No current cigarette smoking across any grade. Current vaping and tobacco nicotine use both at 6% among 11th graders. Zero current use in Gr. 7 and Gr. 9.
Grade 5 tobacco/vaping norms & behaviors — ESU (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified Strong anti-tobacco norms (94% think smoking "very bad") but early exposure exists: 6% have ever vaped and 13% report friends who vape. 66% say their class discussed smoking dangers; 56% discussed vaping harms — indicating some curriculum coverage. n=32.
Perceived harm of tobacco/vaping — Gr. 11 ESU vs. younger grades
Eastern Sierra Unified % rating "great harm." Occasional vaping is less recognized as harmful than daily use — especially among younger students. Prevention messaging gap identified around light/occasional use. Source: CHKS 2023–24 Secondary.
ESU Gr. 11 lifetime vaping vs. California & national comparisons
Eastern Sierra Unified California statewide ESU 11th grade lifetime vaping (28%) is double the CA 9–12 current vaping rate (~14%) and nearly triple the national high school current vaping rate (~10%). Note: ESU figure is "lifetime" use, CA/national figures are "current" (past 30 days) — direct comparison overstates the gap but the magnitude is still noteworthy. Sources: CHKS 2023–24; CDPH CYTS 2022; CDC NYTS 2023.
Perceived difficulty getting tobacco/vaping — by grade (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified % rating "very difficult" to obtain. 56% of 11th graders say cigarettes or vapes are "fairly/very easy" to get — suggesting enforcement and access gaps by late secondary school. Aligns with retailer proximity data.
School anti-tobacco policy awareness — by grade (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified % who agree school bans tobacco use and vaping. Lower awareness among 9th graders (54%) vs. 7th (73%) and 11th (83%) — a mid-secondary messaging gap. No students in any grade reported secondhand smoke on school grounds in past 30 days.

Cigarette quit ratio — regional estimate vs. California average
Regional estimate California statewide Quit ratio = former smokers ÷ (current + former smokers). A higher ratio means a greater share of people who ever smoked have successfully quit. The regional estimate (~58%) is below the CA average (~62%), suggesting a cessation gap. Mono is grouped with 6 other small counties; Mono-specific data not available. Source: CHIS 2022–23, CDPH County Dashboard.

Secondhand smoke/vape exposure past 2 weeks — adults (CA context)
Regional estimate California statewide Secondhand exposure remains common; vaping has expanded secondhand exposure in settings previously smoke-free. CA Air Resources Board classifies secondhand smoke as a toxic air contaminant equivalent to diesel exhaust. Source: Online CATS 2023; CHIS 2022–23.
School-based secondhand exposure — ESU secondary students (CHKS 2023–24)
Eastern Sierra Unified Zero students in Grades 7, 9, or 11 reported secondhand smoke exposure on school grounds in the past 30 days — a positive indicator of effective on-campus policy enforcement.
Policy context: A 2016 Mono County Public Health Department survey of 184 unincorporated Mono adults found 62% supported a ban on flavored tobacco sales. A survey of 70 unincorporated businesses found majority support for smoke-free outdoor dining and 20-foot smoke-free doorway ordinances. California has since banned most flavored tobacco products statewide (Health & Safety Code 104559.5). Mono County can adopt additional local policies on smoke-free public spaces, tobacco retail licensing, and proximity restrictions near schools.

Mono County: schools near tobacco retailers (CTHAT 2025)
Mono County direct Data from CTHAT (California Tobacco Health Assessment Tool), Stanford Prevention Research Center, using CDTFA licensed tobacco retailers (2025) and CA School Campus Database (2025). 75% of Mono County K–12 schools have at least one licensed tobacco retailer within a 1,000-foot radius — meaning the majority of students pass by a tobacco point-of-sale on their route to school. Research links retailer density near schools to increased youth tobacco initiation and experimentation.
Underage tobacco sales violation rate — CA region comparison (2024 STPS)
Regional estimate Mono County is likely included in the "High Country/North Valley" region, which has the lowest violation rate statewide (7.2%). The Synar survey does not report Mono County individually. Statewide rate: 18.9%. Federal requirement: ≤20% to preserve SABG funding. Source: 2024 CA Synar Tobacco Purchase Survey, CDPH.
Local tobacco policy coverage — Mono County (CTHAT / PETS 2024)
Mono County Local policy status from CTHAT/PETS (American Nonsmokers' Rights Foundation, October 2024). "Population covered" = % of county population under each local policy. Statewide flavored tobacco ban applies; local adoption of additional policies (TRL, smoke-free housing) varies. Source: CTHAT 2025.
Enforcement context: The STAKE Act prohibits tobacco sales to anyone under 21 and requires retailers to post 1-800-STAKE signs. The 2024 STPS found 24.2% of CA retailers statewide did not check ID — when no ID is checked, the violation rate jumps to 70.6% vs. 2.4% when ID is verified. California's SB 1230 (2024) increased penalties for both STAKE Act violations and the flavored tobacco sales ban. ESU student data shows 56% of 11th graders perceive tobacco/vapes as "fairly/very easy to get" — consistent with the retailer-proximity pattern and warranting continued local enforcement focus.
